How to calculate daily caloric intake?

Any diet, regardless of the set goal, begins with calculating daily caloric, protein, fat, and carbohydrate requirements. In this article, we will discuss the most accurate methods for determining daily caloric intake for both men and women.

What is basal metabolism?

The calculation of daily caloric intake begins with determining the basal metabolic rate (BMR) â€“ the amount of energy required by the body to sustain essential life processes. Even at rest, the human body constantly expends calories on activities such as breathing, digestion, circulation, and other physiological processes. Daily caloric intake should exceed the BMR, otherwise, the body cannot function normally. Basal metabolic rate can be determined through two methods: direct and indirect. In the first method, an individual is placed in a special chamber where the amount of heat they generate is measured, and the BMR is then calculated. This method is the most accurate but is less accessible. The indirect method involves calculating the basal metabolic rate using a specific formula.
